Spectral Fingerprinting

NIR, Raman, and UV-Vis spectroscopy together form a layered phytochemical verification system beyond taste profiling alone.

Near-Infrared (NIR)

Detects overtones of C–H, O–H, and N–H bonds, enabling quantification of alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides without sample destruction.

Raman Spectroscopy

Provides complementary molecular fingerprinting through inelastic light scattering, particularly effective for aromatic compounds and terpenes.

UV-Visible Integration

Chromophoric compound detection for phenolics, flavonoids, and carotenoids with precise λmax alignment to reference standards.

Chemometric Modelling

PCA, PLS-DA, and neural network fusion of spectral and taste data yields identification accuracy exceeding 96% on test sets — all computed locally.
